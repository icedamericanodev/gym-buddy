How is this calculated? (sources)
  • BMI — weight (kg) / height (m)². Categories: underweight <18.5, healthy 18.5–24.9, overweight 25–29.9, obese ≥30. Source: World Health Organization.
  • BMR — Mifflin-St Jeor equation: 10·weight + 6.25·height − 5·age + (5 for men, −161 for women). Source: Mifflin et al. (1990); validated as most accurate by Frankenfield et al., American Dietetic Association (2005).
  • TDEE — BMR × activity factor (1.2 sedentary → 1.9 athlete). Harris-Benedict multipliers; same convention used by the FAO/WHO/UNU.
  • Daily target — −500 kcal/day for a ~0.5 kg/week cut (NIH guidance on sustainable weight loss); +300 kcal/day for gradual lean gain (Iraki et al., 2019; Aragon et al., 2017).
  • Protein — goal-dependent: 2.2 g/kg cut, 1.6 g/kg maintain, 1.8 g/kg gain. Sources: ISSN Position Stand (Jäger et al., 2017); Morton et al. meta-analysis (2018) identified ~1.6 g/kg as the point of diminishing returns for hypertrophy; cut value sits at the upper end of the range to spare lean mass in deficit (Helms et al., 2014).
  • Fat — 25% of daily calories. Within the Institute of Medicine Acceptable Macronutrient Distribution Range (AMDR: 20–35%).
  • Carbs — remainder of calories after protein and fat. Typically within the IOM AMDR (45–65%); aggressive cuts may fall below.
  • Water — 35 mL per kg body weight (EFSA 2010 adult reference; consistent with the Holliday-Segar derivation of 30–40 mL/kg). The IOM (2005) Dietary Reference Intake suggests ~3.7 L/day total water for men and ~2.7 L for women, of which ~20% comes from food.

These are general adult guidelines, not medical advice. Talk to a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized recommendations, especially if you have a medical condition.
